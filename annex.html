Annex #1 - What is Cash?

Cash is a term used to describe physical currency, such as coins and banknotes, that is legal tender and used as a medium of exchange for goods and services. It is a widely accepted form of payment and is used in almost every country around the world.

Cash refers to physical currency, which includes coins and paper money issued by a government or central bank. It is a tangible form of money that people use for everyday transactions.

Key Characteristics of Cash

  • Physical Form: Unlike digital money or bank deposits, cash is a physical asset that can be held and exchanged directly between individuals.
  • Immediate Finality: Cash transactions are settled instantly. Once exchanged, the payment is final, without the need for any third-party verification, such as a bank or payment processor.
  • No Dependency on Technology: Cash doesn’t require electricity, internet, or electronic systems to function, making it usable in areas or situations where digital payment methods are unavailable.
  • Anonymity: Cash transactions offer a degree of privacy because they don’t leave a digital trail. Other forms of money, such as credit cards, bank transfers, or cryptocurrencies, are usually traceable.
  • Universal Acceptance: Cash is widely accepted and recognized in the issuing country. Other types of money, like checks or digital payments, may be limited by geographic or institutional factors.

While cash has these distinct features, the rise of digital payment methods, credit cards, and cryptocurrencies has increased the use of other forms of money, but cash remains a fundamental medium of exchange.
